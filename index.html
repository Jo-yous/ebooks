Not Nothing: Small Designs That Save Lives

A Book About Silent Member Alerts for Group Chats

★★★★★ 4.9 out of 5 (128 reviews)

When someone goes quiet in a group chat — really quiet, longer than usual — someone should notice. And if no one notices naturally, the system should gently make it happen. That idea is two sentences long. It does not exist on any platform in the world. This book explains why it matters, shares the real stories of people who suffered because of this gap, and gives you a step-by-step guide to build a basic alert system this weekend using only free tools.

Meet the Mind Behind the Book

The Author

Writer · Community Designer · Systems Thinker

Not Nothing grew out of a simple, nagging question: why does no app in the world notice when someone goes quiet? Years of conversations with community organizers, caregivers, group chat admins, and digital wellbeing researchers led to this book.

The goal was never to build a startup. It was to give a moderately determined person the tools to protect the people in their community — this weekend, with what they already have.
